Abrin
Abrin occurs in the the rosary pea (jequirity pea), (Abrus precatorius) Abrin, acts as a ribosome-inhibiting protein (RIP) and is a lethal poison.
It is a yellow-white powder and is soluble in solutions of sodium chloride, usually with turbidity.
Toxic proteins of abrin are resistant to digestive enzymes but are destroyed in cooking.
The toxic portion is heat stable to 60 °C for 30 minutes, but at 80 °C most of the toxicity is lost in 30 minutes.
Hazardous polymerization will not occur and it is stable in the environment.
Abrin-a, 1 of the 4 isoabrins from a plant, has the highest inhibitory effect on protein synthesis.
It consists of an A chain of 250 amino acids and a B chain of 267 amino acids.
