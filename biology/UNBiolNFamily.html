9.1.0 Latin names of plants
Carl Linnaeus (1707-1778) invented a method for classifying and ranking living organisms
In 1735, he published it in his book Systema Naturae, written mainly in Latin
He gave each species of plant a binomial name.
For example, he called the garden pea (Pisum satisum), (Latin satisum satisfaction)
Family: Fabaceae, (Leguminosae) pea family
A plant name appears as upper-case and lower-case, and italics are recommended.
Family is a basic taxonomic grouping ranking above genus and below order.
Family names end in -aceae, except for some older names, e.g. the former Leguminosae above.
9.2.0 Genus
It appears as upper-case initial letter and italics are recommended.
It is a noun with gender masculine or feminine or neuter
The plural term is genera
If the same genus is mentioned, then following the first name the following name is abbreviated, e.g. P. sativum.
However, in the internet the genus by itself is shown as the word, Pisum.
This is the methos used in this publication.
9.3.0 Species
It appears in lower case: sativum
It is usually an adjective
The binomial combination of "genus + species" gives the name of the plant, e.g. (Pisum sativum)
If a species is not known, use just "Pisum".
If referring to all the species in a genus, use Pisum
In this document, the following are not needed: "Pisum sp." or "Pisum species".
A species first named by Carl Linnaeus, 1707 - 1778, may be still named as follows: forage pea (Pisum arvense L.)
9.4.0 Subspecies
It appears as lower case.
It is preceded by "subsp." in lower case Roman type.
9.5.0 Variety
Other plants in the species or subspecies differ in minor, but heritable characters.
It appears as lower case.
It is preceded by "var." in lower case Roman type, e.g. the snow pea (Pisum sativum var. saccharatum)
9.6.0 Cultivar
It appears as upper-case and lower-case Roman type with single quotation marks.
"Grevillea 'Robyn Gordon', is a man-made hybrid between two species."
Asparagus bean, Vigna unguiculata 'Sesquipedalis'
9.7.0 Name of plant
The names of plants is governed by the last revision of the International Code of Botanical Nomenclature, based on its original description.
This method dates back to 1735, when Carl Linneaus published his idea of a naming system for plants.
